How it works

Three quiet steps. Most of the time, nothing happens.

  1. 1

    Watch detects a possible fall

    Fall Guardian monitors motion on a paired Apple Watch. A suspected fall triggers a silent alert on the assisted user's phone.

  2. 2

    User can cancel within 30 seconds

    If everything is fine, a single tap cancels the alert. No caregiver is disturbed. False alarms stay private.

  3. 3

    Caregiver receives an alert

    If the countdown expires uncancelled, the linked caregiver receives a push notification and can acknowledge — bringing calm, attentive response without calling emergency services.

Designed around the caregiver

Caregivers see a clear notification with context — who, when, and whether it was acknowledged. No missed calls, no guesswork.

Frequently asked questions

No. Fall Guardian notifies a linked caregiver when a fall alert goes unanswered. It is not a certified medical device and not a substitute for emergency services. In an emergency, call 15 (SAMU), 18 (Sapeurs-Pompiers), or 112 (European emergency number).

Currently Fall Guardian requires an Apple Watch paired with an iPhone for the assisted user, and an iPhone for the caregiver. Android support is under consideration for a later phase.

Fall detection relies on Apple Watch motion sensors. Like any motion-based system, it can produce false alerts — for example from vigorous exercise or dropping the watch. The 30-second cancellation window handles these cases, and detection accuracy is continuously reviewed during the private preview.

Alert data is shared only with the caregiver you explicitly link in the app. We do not sell personal data. See our privacy notice for full details on data handling, processors, and your rights.
